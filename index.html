I build things end-to-end. ML pipelines that don't just train a model but handle data ingestion, preprocessing, feature engineering, and evaluation properly. Systems that solve a specific real problem — predicting customer churn, optimizing traffic flow in a city I actually live in, classifying EEG signals to give someone a voice. The math has to work, and the output has to be useful.

The problems I find interesting are the ones that don't have clean textbook answers. Real-world data is messy, imbalanced, and seasonally structured. Optimization problems have conflicting objectives and constraints that don't fit neatly into a single metric. I like the part where you have to think carefully rather than just apply a method.

I'm looking for internships, research collaborations, and project partnerships in ML, data engineering, or full-stack development. If you're building something interesting and need an extra pair of hands who will show up, think carefully, and ship things — reach out.
